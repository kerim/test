If you walk around Taipei these days you’ll be sure to see the word KUSO written in big letters all over the place. For instance, this summer there was a city-sponsored cultural festival in Ximending called “<a href="http://flickr.com/photos/yc_chen/712974902/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://flickr.com/photos/yc_chen/712974902/', 'KUSO Ximending']);"  target="_blank">KUSO Ximending</a>.”

But what does “KUSO” mean? Luckily, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuso" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuso', 'Wikipedia']);"  target="_blank">Wikipedia</a> is there to help us out:

> **Kuso** is the term used in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_world" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_world', 'Chinese world']);"  target="_blank" title="Chinese world">Chinese world</a> for the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_culture" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_culture', 'internet culture']);"  target="_blank" title="Internet culture">internet culture</a> that generally includes all types of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Camp_%28style%29" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Camp_%28style%29', 'camp']);"  target="_blank" title="Camp (style)">camp</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parody" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parody', 'parody']);"  target="_blank" title="Parody">parody</a>. The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandarin_Chinese" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandarin_Chinese', 'Mandarin Chinese']);"  target="_blank" title="Mandarin Chinese">Mandarin Chinese</a> word *ègǎo* (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_character" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_character', 'simplified Chinese']);"  target="_blank" title="Simplified Chinese character">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans">恶搞</span>; <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_character" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_character', 'traditional Chinese']);"  target="_blank" title="Traditional Chinese character">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant">惡搞</span>, literally meaning “reckless doings”) is often used as a synonym or description of its meaning. In <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_language" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_language', 'Japanese']);"  target="_blank" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>, kuso <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span lang="ja">糞,くそ</span><span><span>,</span> <em><span>kuso</span></em></span><span><sup><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:Japanese" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:Japanese', '?']);"  target="_blank" title="Help:Japanese"><span style="padding: 0pt 0.1em; color: #0000ee; font-family: sans-serif; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: bold; font-size: 80%; line-height: normal; font-size-adjust: none; font-stretch: normal; text-decoration: none">?</span></a></sup></span>)</span> means <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shit" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shit', 'shit']);"  target="_blank" title="Shit">shit</a>, and is often uttered as an interjection. It is also used to describe outrageous matters and objects of poor quality. This definition of kuso was brought into Taiwan in around <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2000" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2000', '2000']);"  target="_blank" title="2000">2000</a> by young people who frequent Japanese websites and quickly became an <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_phenomenon" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_phenomenon', 'internet phenomenon']);"  target="_blank" title="Internet phenomenon">internet phenomenon</a>, spreading to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hong_Kong" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hong_Kong', 'Hong Kong']);"  target="_blank" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> and subsequently the rest of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China', 'China']);"  target="_blank" title="China">China</a>.

The range of meanings is pretty wide, with some people applying it to anything stupidly-funny, and others restricting it more narrowly to internet and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manga" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manga', 'manga']);"  target="_blank">manga</a> culture. You can get a sense of the range of meanings by looking a <a href="http://flickr.com/search/?q=kuso&s=int&ss=2&ct=6" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://flickr.com/search/?q=kuso&s=int&ss=2&ct=6', 'Flickr photos tagged KUSO']);"  target="_blank">Flickr photos tagged KUSO</a>.
