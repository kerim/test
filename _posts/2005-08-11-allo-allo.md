From *<a href="http://www.csmonitor.com/2005/0811/p01s04-woeu.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.csmonitor.com/2005/0811/p01s04-woeu.html', 'The Christian Science Monitor']);" >The Christian Science Monitor</a>*, English is increasingly the language of French businesses:

> In a recent survey of 26 of France&#8217;s largest companies, 16 gave English as their official working language &#8211; including Renault, Danone, and Aventis. Of these, nine have dropped French altogether. Seven put English and French on equal footing.

The French, of course, won&#8217;t give up their language without a fight:

> not all French institutions are embracing the trend. Last year, labor unions at General Electric Medical Systems in France challenged in court the company&#8217;s English-only manuals. They won under a French law that mandates all foreign terms used in the workplace be translated into French.
